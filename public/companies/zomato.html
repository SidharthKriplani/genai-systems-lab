How Zomato Interviews for AI/ML Roles

Zomato hires data scientists / ML engineers for consumer-scale food-delivery problems (ranking, ETA, demand, fraud). The loop is a fairly classic Indian-startup shape: an online assessment (ML MCQs + DSA), then 2-3 technical interviews mixing classical ML, DL, SQL/DSA, project deep-dives and an ML-framing-for-a-business-problem round, plus a culture/fit chat. Depth varies by team and level.

The Interview Loop

  1. Online assessment — HackerRank-style: 8-15 MCQs on ML, statistics, SQL and basic math, plus 1-2 DSA coding problems (often a DP or medium-hard question).
  2. Technical round 1 (~50 min) — Classical ML (bias/variance, class imbalance, correlation, EDA), DL basics (feedforward, backprop, dropout, activations), and optimisation, often tied to a Zomato-flavoured problem.
  3. Technical round 2 (~50 min) — Framing an ML solution for a business problem end-to-end; may extend into PySpark coding, SQL, and more DSA/ML/DS depending on the role.
  4. Project / resume deep-dive — Drilling into your past work and CV projects: why you chose a model, feature and loss decisions, and what you would do differently.
  5. Hiring manager / culture round — Fit, ownership, ways of working, plus logistics and compensation discussion.

What They Weight

  • Classical ML fundamentals explained from first principles
  • Framing a business problem as an ML system end-to-end
  • SQL and DSA fluency (Python; sometimes PySpark)
  • Consumer-scale intuition: ranking, ETA, demand, fraud
  • Clear reasoning about your own project decisions

Focus Your Prep On

  • Classical ML: bias-variance, regularisation, class imbalance, tree ensembles
  • Deep learning basics: feedforward/backprop, dropout, activations, optimisers
  • SQL and data manipulation (incl. PySpark for some teams)
  • DSA at LeetCode-medium (arrays, strings, DP)
  • Probability, statistics and EDA on messy real data
  • Applied recommendation / ranking / ETA / fraud framing

Prep angle: Be able to explain your best algorithm from scratch with the math, and to frame a Zomato-style problem (ranking, ETA, demand, fraud) as a full ML pipeline. Drill ML/stats MCQs, SQL and LeetCode-medium DSA in Python (touch PySpark if the JD mentions it), and prepare tight project stories where you can defend every modeling choice.
