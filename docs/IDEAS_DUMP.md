## AI Bookshelf / AI Reading Radar

**Status:** Idea only. Do not implement yet.

**Concept:** A small curated AI/ML/GenAI reading radar that helps users discover important AI-related books and understand which ones are worth reading for their goals.

**Sharp product angle:**
> "Which AI book should I read for my goal?"

**Possible user goals:**
- I am a software engineer learning LLM systems.
- I am a PM trying to understand AI products.
- I am preparing for AI/ML interviews.
- I want foundations, not hype.
- I want AI product strategy, not math.
- I want system design / RAG / agent infrastructure depth.

**Possible features:**
- Curated book cards with concise overview
- Who the book is for
- Difficulty level and practical usefulness rating
- Key concepts covered
- "Read this if…" guidance
- Suggested reading paths by goal
- Links into relevant GenAI Systems Lab modules
- Later: chapter notes, concept maps, role-based reading tracks

**Potential fit:** Could become a "Reading Radar" or "Bookshelf" section inside GenAI Systems Lab, but only after the core lab experience is stable. It should support the lab, not distract from it.
